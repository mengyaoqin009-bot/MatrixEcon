II. Compensation peer / peer group variables

> **中文：** II. 薪酬同业公司／同业组变量

Dispersion in relative book values  
Standard deviation of the relative book values in a focal firm’s compensation peer group. Relative book value is the ratio of a peer’s total book value of assets to the total book value of assets of the focal firm. The measure is winsorized at the 1st and 99th percentiles.  
Compustat

> **中文：**  
> 相对账面价值离散度  
> 核心公司薪酬同业组中相对账面价值的标准差。相对账面价值是同业公司的资产账面总值与核心公司的资产账面总值之比。该指标在第 1 和第 99 百分位数处进行缩尾处理。  
> Compustat

Dispersion in relative market values  
Standard deviation of the relative market values in a focal firm’s compensation peer group. Relative market value is the ratio of peer’s market value of assets (defined as total book value of assets minus book value of equity plus market value of equity at fiscal year end) to the market value of assets of the focal firm. The measure is winsorized at the 1st and 99th percentiles.  
Compustat

> **中文：**  
> 相对市场价值离散度  
> 核心公司薪酬同业组中相对市场价值的标准差。相对市场价值是同业公司的资产市场价值与核心公司的资产市场价值之比，其中资产市场价值定义为资产账面总值减去权益账面价值，再加上财政年度末的权益市场价值。该指标在第 1 和第 99 百分位数处进行缩尾处理。  
> Compustat

Deviation from industry-size benchmarking  
Fraction of compensation peers that are from outside the GICS1 industry or that are much larger (>200%) or much smaller (<50%) than the focal firm in book or market value of assets. The measure is winsorized at the 1st and 99th percentiles.  
Compustat

> **中文：**  
> 偏离行业-规模基准比较  
> 薪酬同业公司中不属于同一 GICS1 行业，或者其资产账面价值或市场价值远大于（>200%）或远小于（<50%）核心公司的公司占比。该指标在第 1 和第 99 百分位数处进行缩尾处理。  
> Compustat

ISS conformity  
Fraction of firm’s actual compensation peers that are included in a synthetic compensation peer group for the firm based on ISS’s peer selection methodology (see Appendix B for details). The measure is winsorized at the 1st and 99th percentiles.  
AuditAnalytics; ISS IncentiveLab; Equilar; Compustat, CRSP

> **中文：**  
> ISS 一致度  
> 公司实际薪酬同业公司中，按照 ISS 的同业公司选择方法被纳入该公司合成薪酬同业组的公司占比（详见附录 B）。该指标在第 1 和第 99 百分位数处进行缩尾处理。  
> AuditAnalytics；ISS IncentiveLab；Equilar；Compustat；CRSP

Fraction of reciprocal peers  
Fraction of compensation peers that reference back the focal firm, winsorized at the 1st and 99th percentiles.  
AuditAnalytics; ISS IncentiveLab; Equilar

> **中文：**  
> 互惠同业公司占比  
> 反向将核心公司列为参照对象的薪酬同业公司占比，在第 1 和第 99 百分位数处进行缩尾处理。  
> AuditAnalytics；ISS IncentiveLab；Equilar

Peer group reciprocity  
The fraction of the existing network linkages that are bilateral (i.e., point in both directions). At the firm-level, the network for a given firm is created by first adding the focal firm and all its compensation peers as nodes, and then by adding all the references between the focal firm and all its peers and the references among the peers as defined in their respective compensation peer groups. The measure is winsorized at the 1st and 99th percentiles.  
AuditAnalytics; ISS IncentiveLab; Equilar

> **中文：**  
> 同业组互惠性  
> 现有网络连接中双向连接（即指向两个方向）所占的比例。在公司层面，首先将核心公司及其所有薪酬同业公司添加为节点，然后根据各自薪酬同业组的定义，添加核心公司与其所有同业公司之间的全部参照关系以及同业公司之间的参照关系，由此构建给定公司的网络。该指标在第 1 和第 99 百分位数处进行缩尾处理。  
> AuditAnalytics；ISS IncentiveLab；Equilar

Peer group transitivity  
The number of triangles divided by the number of triads that exist in a network. Triads are groups of three nodes that are connected in some way; triangles are triads that are completely connected. A higher number indicates a greater connectivity among the members of the network. The measure is winsorized at the 1st and 99th percentiles.  
AuditAnalytics; ISS IncentiveLab; Equilar

> **中文：**  
> 同业组传递性  
> 网络中的三角形数量除以现有三元组数量。三元组是以某种方式相互连接的三个节点组成的集合；三角形则是完全连接的三元组。数值越高，表示网络成员之间的连接程度越高。该指标在第 1 和第 99 百分位数处进行缩尾处理。  
> AuditAnalytics；ISS IncentiveLab；Equilar

Peer group cluster coefficient  
The number of triangles divided by the number of possible triads. A higher number indicates a more complete graph. The measure is winsorized at the 1st and 99th percentiles.  
AuditAnalytics; ISS IncentiveLab; Equilar

> **中文：**  
> 同业组聚类系数  
> 三角形数量除以可能形成的三元组数量。数值越高，表示网络图越完整。该指标在第 1 和第 99 百分位数处进行缩尾处理。  
> AuditAnalytics；ISS IncentiveLab；Equilar

Low initial conformity  
An indicator variable that is one for firms with below-median conformity with the industry-size peer benchmark in 2007, else 0. Conformity with the industry-size benchmark is measured as the fraction of compensation peers that are from the same GICS2 industry and that are within 50-200% of a firm’s relative market value of assets.  
Compustat; CRSP; AuditAnalytics; ISS IncentiveLab; Equilar

> **中文：**  
> 初始一致度较低  
> 指示变量：若公司在 2007 年与行业-规模同业基准的一致度低于中位数，则取值为 1，否则为 0。与行业-规模基准的一致度，以薪酬同业公司中属于同一 GICS2 行业且资产市场价值处于该公司资产市场价值 50%—200% 范围内的公司占比衡量。  
> Compustat；CRSP；AuditAnalytics；ISS IncentiveLab；Equilar

ISS rank  
Rank that ISS assigns to all potential same-GICS2 industry peers based on its peer selection methodology (see Appendix B for details).  
Compustat; CRSP

> **中文：**  
> ISS 排名  
> ISS 根据其同业公司选择方法对属于同一 GICS2 行业的所有潜在同业公司赋予的排名（详见附录 B）。  
> Compustat；CRSP

Included in peer group  
Indicator variable that is one if a firm is part of the actual compensation peer group of a given firm.  
Compustat; AuditAnalytics; ISS IncentiveLab; Equilar

> **中文：**  
> 纳入同业组  
> 指示变量：若某公司是给定公司实际薪酬同业组的成员，则取值为 1。  
> Compustat；AuditAnalytics；ISS IncentiveLab；Equilar

Relative market value  
Market value of assets of peer scaled by focal firm’s market value of assets, winsorized at the 1st and 99th percentiles.  
Compustat

> **中文：**  
> 相对市场价值  
> 同业公司的资产市场价值除以核心公司的资产市场价值，并在第 1 和第 99 百分位数处进行缩尾处理。  
> Compustat

Relative Tobin’s Q  
Tobin’s Q of peer scaled by focal firm’s Tobin’s Q, winsorized at the 1st and 99th percentiles.  
Compustat

> **中文：**  
> 相对 Tobin’s Q  
> 同业公司的 Tobin’s Q 除以核心公司的 Tobin’s Q，并在第 1 和第 99 百分位数处进行缩尾处理。  
> Compustat

Relative sales  
Total sales of peer scaled by focal firm’s total sales, winsorized at the 1st and 99th percentiles.  
Compustat

Fraction shares owned by index mutual funds  
Defined as in Appel, Gormley and Keim (2016), p.116: The fraction of a firm’s shares that are owned by mutual funds that are flagged as passively managed. A mutual fund is flagged as passively managed if its fund name includes a string that identifies it as an index fund. The strings used to identify index funds are: Index, Idx, Indx, Ind (where indicates a space), Russell, S & P, S and P, S&P, SandP, SP, DOW, Dow, DJ, MSCI, Bloomberg, KBW, NASDAQ, NYSE, STOXX, FTSE, Wilshire, Morningstar, 100, 400, 500, 600, 900, 1000, 1500, 2000, and 5000. The measure is winsorized at the 5th and 95th percentiles.  
Refinitiv 13F holdings; CRSP mutual fund

> **中文：**  
> 指数共同基金持股比例  
> 按 Appel, Gormley and Keim (2016), p.116 的定义：被标记为被动管理型共同基金持有的公司股份占比。如果共同基金的基金名称包含能够识别其为指数基金的字符串，则将该基金标记为被动管理型。用于识别指数基金的字符串包括：Index、Idx、Indx、Ind（其中表示一个空格）、Russell、S & P、S and P、S&P、SandP、SP、DOW、Dow、DJ、MSCI、Bloomberg、KBW、NASDAQ、NYSE、STOXX、FTSE、Wilshire、Morningstar、100、400、500、600、900、1000、1500、2000 和 5000。该指标在第 5 和第 95 百分位数处进行缩尾处理。  
> Refinitiv 13F holdings；CRSP mutual fund

Propensity to vote with ISS  
The fraction of times that a firm’s mutual fund investors voted alongside ISS in prior years when ISS and the firm management disagreed on the vote recommendation, averaged across all shareholdings, with each fund weighted by the fraction of shares it owns in the firm. (Data availability starts in 2003 and ends in 2017.) The measure is winsorized at the 5th and 95th percentiles.  
Refinitiv 13F holdings; ISS voting analytics; ISS mutual fund voting

> **中文：**  
> 与 ISS 一致投票的倾向  
> 在以往年度 ISS 与公司管理层的投票建议不一致时，公司共同基金投资者与 ISS 一致投票的次数占比。该比例在全部持股中取加权平均，每只基金的权重为其持有的公司股份比例。（数据可得期间始于 2003 年，止于 2017 年。）该指标在第 5 和第 95 百分位数处进行缩尾处理。

Appendix B: ISS’s methodology for the selection of compensation peers

> **中文：** 附录 B：ISS 选择薪酬同业公司的方法

On December 20, 2011, ISS published a whitepaper that included a new approach to establish peer groups that are used for compensation analysis and pay-for-performance screens (Gibson, 2011). The whitepaper was republished with minor revisions on February 17, 2012 (ISS, 2012a).

> **中文：** 2011 年 12 月 20 日，ISS 发布了一份白皮书，其中提出了一种建立用于薪酬分析和薪酬绩效筛选的同业组的新方法（Gibson, 2011）。该白皮书经过小幅修订后，于 2012 年 2 月 17 日再次发布（ISS, 2012a）。

In that whitepaper, ISS disclosed its new methodology for the selection of a comparison peer group that consists of the following steps:

> **中文：** 在该白皮书中，ISS 披露了其选择比较同业组的新方法，该方法包括以下步骤：

1. Create an initial list of potential peers from the two-digit GICS universe as the subject company that satisfies the following two criteria:

> **中文：** 1. 从与被评估公司相同的两位数 GICS 行业范围内创建潜在同业公司的初始名单，并要求其满足以下两个标准：

(cid:136) potential peers are between 0.45 and 2.1 times the subject company’s annual sales (assets for financial firms).

> **中文：** (cid:136) 潜在同业公司的年度销售额应介于被评估公司年度销售额的 0.45 倍至 2.1 倍之间（金融公司采用资产衡量）。

(cid:136) potential peers have a market capitalization of between 0.2 times and 5 times the subject company’s market capitalization.

> **中文：** (cid:136) 潜在同业公司的市值应介于被评估公司市值的 0.2 倍至 5 倍之间。

2. Narrow down the initial list of potential peers to those companies that are in the subject company’s six-digit GICS category and rank them from 1 to N using the absolute relative distance in sales so that the subject company stays near the median of the selected group.

> **中文：** 2. 将潜在同业公司的初始名单缩小至与被评估公司属于同一六位数 GICS 类别的公司，并依据销售额的绝对相对距离将其从 1 排至 N，以使被评估公司保持在所选同业组中位数附近。

3. Take the top 24 potential peers for the subject firm’s peer group. If this peer group has fewer than 14 peers, then supplement peers by repeating step (ii) for firms in the subject firm’s four-digit GICS. If thereafter there are still fewer than 14 peers, repeat step (ii) for firms in the subject firm’s two-digit GICS.

> **中文：** 3. 选取排名前 24 位的潜在同业公司组成被评估公司的同业组。如果该同业组少于 14 家同业公司，则针对与被评估公司属于同一四位数 GICS 类别的公司重复步骤 (ii)，以补充同业公司。此后若同业公司仍少于 14 家，则针对与被评估公司属于同一两位数 GICS 类别的公司重复步骤 (ii)。

In some rare cases, firms do not end up with 14 peers. An example of this are companies that much larger than others in its industry (referred to as “super-mega non-financial companies” by ISS); in such cases, realizing the importance of firm size on CEO pay, ISS relaxes the industry criterion so to pair these firms with peers that are similar-sized.

> **中文：** 在少数情况下，公司最终无法获得 14 家同业公司。例如，有些公司远大于其所在行业的其他公司（ISS 将其称为“超巨型非金融公司”）；在这种情况下，鉴于公司规模对 CEO 薪酬的重要性，ISS 会放宽行业标准，以便将这些公司与规模相近的同业公司进行配对。

Over the years, ISS has made some modifications to its selection methodology. Some of the changes added complexity while not truly affecting the industry and size match outcomes. For example, one such change was to tighten the initial industry selection filter from GICS6 to GICS8 (if insufficient peers are identified, this was again relaxed to GICS6, GICS4 and GICS2 as described in step (iii) above). Another change allowed firms from the GICS8 industry of the subject firm’s own chosen compensation peers as potential peers with some priority given to what ISS calls “first-degree peers” (those that referenced back the subject firm) and those with numerous connections (i.e. those often chosen as a peer).

> **中文：** 多年来，ISS 对其选择方法进行了一些调整。部分调整增加了方法的复杂性，但并未实质性影响行业和规模匹配结果。例如，其中一项调整是将初始行业筛选条件从 GICS6 收紧至 GICS8（如果识别出的同业公司数量不足，则按照上述步骤 (iii) 的说明，依次放宽至 GICS6、GICS4 和 GICS2）。另一项调整允许将被评估公司自行选择的薪酬同业公司所属 GICS8 行业中的公司纳入潜在同业公司，并对 ISS 所称的“一级同业公司”（即反向将被评估公司列为参照对象的公司）以及拥有大量连接的公司（即经常被选为同业公司的公司）给予一定优先权。

Despite such changes, repeated disclosures by ISS describing analyses of their peer group method show that there are little effective changes to the tightness of the industry-size restrictions. For example, in 2012, ISS reports that for the 2013 proxy season, 90 percent of peer groups maintain the subject company within 20 percent of the peer group median size and no peer groups had 2-digit GICS peers (ISS, 2012b, 15). In 2019, ISS reports a 2017 analysis that there were still no 2-digit GICS peers and 85 percent of peer groups maintain the subject firm within 20 percent of the peer group median size (ISS, 2019a, 3). In 2023, it reports the same statistics as in 2019 (ISS, 2023, 4-5).

> **中文：** 尽管存在上述调整，ISS 多次披露的同业组方法分析结果表明，行业-规模限制的严格程度实际上几乎没有变化。例如，ISS 在 2012 年报告称，在 2013 年委托书季，90% 的同业组使被评估公司的规模保持在同业组规模中位数上下 20% 的范围内，并且没有任何同业组包含两位数 GICS 同业公司（ISS, 2012b, 15）。2019 年，ISS 报告了一项 2017 年的分析，结果显示仍然没有两位数 GICS 同业公司，并且 85% 的同业组使被评估公司的规模保持在同业组规模中位数上下 20% 的范围内（ISS, 2019a, 3）。2023 年，ISS 报告了与 2019 年相同的统计数据（ISS, 2023, 4-5）。
