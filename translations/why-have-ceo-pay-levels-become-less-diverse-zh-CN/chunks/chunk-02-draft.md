## 2.2. Institutional factors contributing to industry-size benchmarking

> **中文：** 2.2. 推动行业-规模基准比较的制度因素

Understanding the institutional factors contributing to industry-size benchmarking requires an analysis of the potential costs and benefits of this practice for investors. On the cost side, the application of industry-size benchmarking across the economy likely introduces the usual disadvantages associated with one-size-fits-all approaches. Notably, to the extent that it constrains the selection of compensation peers, imposing such criterion limits boards’ discretion in tailoring pay policies to firm-specific needs, leading to deviations from optimal pay levels and potentially affecting hiring, retention, and incentives.

> **中文：** 理解推动行业-规模基准比较的制度因素，需要分析这种做法给投资者带来的潜在成本与收益。在成本方面，在整个经济体中应用行业-规模基准比较，可能会带来“一刀切”方法通常具有的弊端。尤其是，由于这一标准会约束薪酬同业公司的选择，强制采用该标准将限制董事会根据公司的特定需求定制薪酬政策的自由裁量权，进而导致薪酬水平偏离最优水平，并可能影响人才招聘、留任和激励。

It could reduce the scope for powerful CEOs to manipulate their own pay (the literature provides some evidence that firms choose compensation peers opportunistically).2 Moreover, even if industry-size benchmarking results in a deviation from optimal peer selection, the cost of such deviation could be lower for investors than the cost of monitoring the benchmarking practices of all portfolio companies. We first conduct a graphical analysis of the evolution of pay dispersion over our sample period. This analysis is conducted at the economy-, industry-size and firm-level. For each year, we measure pay dispersion as the standard deviation of Pay, computed as the CEO’s total compensation (TDC1 in Execucomp) scaled by its sample median in that year. A value of, say, 0.5 (1.5) for Pay hence implies that a CEO receives half (1.5 times) the median CEO pay level in that year. We scale TDC1 by its median because the level of executive pay measured in dollars is non-stationary (it increases over time) and because comparing dispersion across time requires a dimensionless measure (see, e.g., Cox and Lewis, 1966).7 We refer to the standard deviation of median-scaled TDC1 as Pay dispersion.

> **中文：** 我们首先对样本期内薪酬离散度的演变进行图形分析。该分析分别在经济体、行业-规模群组和公司层面开展。对于每一年，我们将薪酬离散度衡量为 `Pay` 的标准差，其中 `Pay` 的计算方式是用当年样本中位数对 CEO 总薪酬（Execucomp 中的 `TDC1`）进行缩放。因此，假设 `Pay` 的取值为 0.5（1.5），则意味着该 CEO 获得的薪酬为当年 CEO 薪酬中位数的一半（1.5 倍）。我们使用中位数对 `TDC1` 进行缩放，是因为以美元计量的高管薪酬水平是非平稳的（其随时间上升），并且跨期比较离散度需要使用无量纲指标（例如，参见 Cox and Lewis, 1966）。7 我们将经中位数缩放的 `TDC1` 的标准差称为 `Pay dispersion`。

7Standard deviation is not a dimensionless measure of dispersion; it increases mechanically with the magnitude of the underlying data (mathematically, SD(c · X) = c · SD(X)). Hence, while the standard deviation may be increasing due to larger magnitudes of the underlying values, actual dispersion declines if the standard deviation does not increase proportionately. For example, median CEO pay in our sample almost quadrupled between 1996 and 2023 (from $1.6 million in 1996 to $6.2 million in 2023 while the standard deviation of CEO pay increased by less than half (from $7.4 million in 1996 to $10.9 million in 2023). We use median pay rather than mean pay for scaling because we are interested in the ‘typical’ CEO’s pay level and not in the aggregate level of CEO pay (see Murphy, 2013, 9). Furthermore, firms often target the median of their compensation peer group and many of ISS’s executive pay screens relate a firm’s pay level to the median of a benchmark group.
