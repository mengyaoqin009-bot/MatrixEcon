## Translation Strategy

- Produce a complete bilingual text rather than a summary.
- Preserve source page markers, section hierarchy, equations, variable names, table and figure identifiers, citations, footnote numbers, and JEL codes.
- Present each source paragraph first and its Chinese translation immediately afterward.
- Keep the reference list in its original language. Translate titles only when needed for comprehension.
- Treat tables as structured content. Translate table titles, panel labels, column headings, notes, and variable descriptions while preserving numbers and significance markers.
- Translate figure titles, legends, and notes. Do not invent values that are not present in extracted text.
- Do not strengthen causal language. Preserve qualifiers such as "suggest," "consistent with," "may," and "likely."

## Main Risks

- "Variation" and "dispersion" are used as near-synonyms; use the terminology above consistently while preserving distinctions in formal variable names.
- "Peer" refers to compensation-benchmarking peers, not merely firms in the same industry.
- "Say on Pay" is a named governance institution and must retain the English term on first occurrence.
- PDF extraction may split words at line endings. Repair only evident line-break hyphenation without altering substantive hyphenated terms.
- Tables may be interleaved or reordered by PDF extraction. Preserve the extracted values and flag genuinely ambiguous structure instead of guessing.
